Deep Learning Model For Detecting Skin Diseases
Skin disease detection is a critical area in healthcare, given the prevalence and impact of skin conditions on individuals' health and quality of life. Skin diseases encompass a wide range of conditions, including dermatitis, eczema, psoriasis, acne, melanoma, and various infections. Accurate and timely detection of skin diseases is crucial for effective treatment and management, as many conditions can worsen if left untreated. Currently, diagnosing skin diseases often relies on manual observation by dermatologists or healthcare professionals, which can be subjective and time-consuming. Moreover, access to specialized healthcare services may be limited, particularly in rural or remote areas. This can result in delayed diagnosis and treatment, leading to potential complications and worsening of the condition. To address these challenges, there is growing interest in developing automated systems for skin disease detection using artificial intelligence (AI) and machine learning (ML) techniques. These systems leverage computer vision algorithms to analyze images of skin lesions and identify potential diseases or abnormalities. By utilizing large datasets of annotated skin images, these models can learn to recognize patterns and features indicative of specific skin conditions.
